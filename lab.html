    • Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse is a 2023 American animated superhero film featuring the Marvel Comics character Miles Morales / Spider-Man, produced by Columbia Pictures and distributed by Sony Pictures Animation in association with Marvel Entertainment. It is a sequel to Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse (2018) and the second film in the Spider-Verse franchise, which is set in a shared multiverse of alternate universes called the "Spider-Verse". The film was directed by Joaquim Dos Santos, Kemp Powers and Justin K. Thompson and written by Phil Lord and Christopher Miller, who both also produced the film alongside Dave Callaham. It stars Shameik Moore, who voices Miles. Other voice actors of the film are Hailee Steinfeld, Brian Tyree Henry, Lauren Vélez, Jake Johnson, Jason Schwartzman, Issa Rae, Karan Soni, Shea Whigham, Greta Lee, Daniel Kaluuya, Mahershala Ali, and Oscar Isaac. Miles goes on an adventure with Gwen Stacy / Spider-Woman (Steinfeld) across the multiverse, where he meets a team of Spider-People led by Miguel O'Hara / Spider-Man 2099 (Isaac) known as the Spider-Society.

    StoryLine

    The storyline of Spider-Man: Across the Spider-Verse centers on Miles Morales as he ventures into the multiverse, encountering numerous versions of Spider-People who are part of a vast Spider-Society. When the inter-dimensional villain "The Spot" emerges, threatening the fabric of the multiverse, Miles is thrust into a high-stakes journey to protect both his world and those beyond. Reunited with Gwen Stacy and guided by Miguel O'Hara (Spider-Man 2099), Miles learns more about the challenges and responsibilities of being a hero as he grapples with his unique sense of duty and morality.

    This sequel takes the themes of heroism further, exploring how the choices of each Spider-Person shape their identity and the multiverse as a whole. The storyline intertwines thrilling action with character depth, allowing the audience to see Miles' journey as both a hero and a teenager balancing family, friends, and the responsibility of his powers. The movie builds on the unique animation style and emotional storytelling of its predecessor, offering a visually stunning and introspective take on the Spider-Man legacy.
